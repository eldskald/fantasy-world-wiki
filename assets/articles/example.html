Example Article

The idea is that you make your map, put a bunch of these links as the names of the places and when you click on them, a wiki-style article shows up explaining the place. The article itself can have links to other articles and other maps, so everything is interlinked and makes for a better reading experience where your players/readers learn about your world in the order they want, by whatever makes them more curious. This exploration/interactive aspect makes it way more fun for them to engage with your world when compared to lore dumping in session or by handing them huge PDFs.

If you want to use this tool to make your own wiki for your world, start by reading this file. You'll need to have a GitHub account and fork this project. More information on the first linked file.

Once you have the initial setup done, you'll have a copy of that whole project running under your name. You can go back here to read the example articles for more information on how to write your own cool ones. You can access this article to learn about making articles, this article to learn about about maps.
